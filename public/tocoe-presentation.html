üéØ The Mission

Why ToCoE Exists

Tables of contents are fundamental to navigating and discovering knowledge, yet comprehensive, machine-readable ToC data barely exists in the public domain.

The Challenge

Libraries and databases have bibliographic records, but detailed tables of contents ‚Äî especially for anthologies, essay collections, and journals ‚Äî are often missing or locked behind commercial databases.

The Solution: Crowdsourcing + Public Domain

ToCoE harnesses volunteer labor to transcribe and structure tables of contents, then releases all data as CC0 (Creative Commons Zero) ‚Äî meaning anyone can use it for any purpose, with no restrictions.

CC0 (Creative Commons Zero): A public domain dedication that waives all copyright and related rights. It's the most permissive way to share data ‚Äî no attribution required, no restrictions whatsoever.

üìñ Understanding the Data Model

FRBR: The Secret Sauce

ToCoE uses the FRBR model to organize bibliographic information precisely and powerfully.

FRBR (Functional Requirements for Bibliographic Records): An international library standard that separates the abstract idea of a creative work from its various editions, translations, and physical copies.

The Four Levels

  1. Work ‚Äî The abstract intellectual content (e.g., Shakespeare's "Hamlet" as a concept)
  2. Expression ‚Äî A specific realization of a Work (e.g., a particular English translation, or the 1603 First Quarto edition)
  3. Manifestation ‚Äî A physical or digital publication (e.g., the 1985 Penguin paperback edition)
  4. Person ‚Äî Creators and contributors (authors, translators, editors)

Why This Matters for ToC

An anthology like "The Best American Essays 2023" contains many separate Works (each essay). FRBR lets us:

  • Track each essay as an independent Work
  • Link essays to their original authors
  • Preserve the anthology's table of contents order
  • Connect to external authority files like Wikidata and the Library of Congress

üîÑ The Workflow

Step 3: Transcribing the ToC

Contributors transcribe the table of contents using a simple markdown format.

Markdown: A lightweight text format that's easy to read and write. Think of it as a simplified way to structure text without complex formatting tools. 
# INTRODUCTION
# THE PERFECT CRITIC
# IMPERFECT CRITICS ‚Äì
## SWINBURNE AS CRITIC
## A ROMANTIC ARISTOCRAT
## THE LOCAL FLAVOUR
# TRADITION AND THE INDIVIDUAL TALENT
# THE POSSIBILITY OF A POETIC DRAMA
# HAMLET AND HIS PROBLEMS
# BEN JONSON

The Syntax Rules

  • # ‚Äî Top-level entry (article, essay, chapter)
  • ## ‚Äî Sub-entry (nested component)
  • # Section Name / ‚Äî Section heading (trailing slash means no entity created)
  • # Title || Author Name ‚Äî Explicit author attribution

Example: # Metaphors We Live By || George Lakoff; Mark Johnson

üîÑ The Workflow

Step 4: Author & Subject Matching

After transcription, contributors link authors and subject headings to authoritative sources through an interactive matching interface.

üë• Multi-Source Person Matching

The platform searches 4 authority sources simultaneously:

  • Local Database: Previously matched authors
  • VIAF: Virtual International Authority File
  • Wikidata: Collaborative knowledge base (Q numbers)
  • Library of Congress: LC Name Authority File

Contributors review suggestions side-by-side and confirm matches with one click. The system distinguishes between authors (linked to Works) and translators (linked to Expressions).

üè∑Ô∏è Intelligent Subject Matching

  • Auto-matching: Imported subjects automatically matched to Library of Congress Subject Headings (LCSH)
  • Bi-directional linking: When LCSH is matched, equivalent Wikidata entities are auto-imported (and vice versa)
  • Manual curation: Contributors can add additional subject headings with verification workflow
  • Subject browser: Explore all subject headings with statistics, search, and popularity rankings
Linked Open Data: By connecting to multiple authority files, ToCoE creates a web of interlinked bibliographic data that's machine-readable and discoverable across systems worldwide.

The Review Process

  3. Verified ToCs are marked as complete and ready for export

This ensures accuracy while keeping the barrier to contribution low.

Comprehensive "Aboutness" Management

ToCoE implements a sophisticated subject heading system that connects works to standardized vocabularies.

üìö Subject Headings Browser

  • Statistics dashboard: Total aboutnesses, verified/proposed counts, unique headings
  • "Most Frequently Linked" ranking: Discover popular subjects
  • Search with autocomplete: Find subject headings quickly
  • Individual subject pages: See all works linked to a subject, with links to Library of Congress and Wikidata

üîÑ Intelligent Auto-Matching

  • Import subjects from book metadata: OpenLibrary and Gutendex subjects automatically imported
  • Exact match detection: Auto-match to Library of Congress Subject Headings (LCSH) via SPARQL queries
  • Bi-directional linking: LCSH ‚Üí auto-import Wikidata entities (P244 property), and vice versa
  • Manual matching with suggestions: For non-exact matches, contributors review and select from candidates
LCSH (Library of Congress Subject Headings): The world's most widely used subject vocabulary. ToCoE connects to the authoritative source via SPARQL, ensuring accuracy and interoperability with library systems worldwide.
